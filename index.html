Frequently Asked Questions
A GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) score measures how visible you are to AI platforms when someone asks about real estate agents in your market. Our tool queries Claude AI to assess your digital footprint across ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Claude — evaluating how much citable, AI-readable content exists about you online. Scores are weighted across all three platforms and range from 0 to 100. Most agents score below 30 on their first scan. That's not a reflection of how good you are at your job — it's a reflection of how little AI-readable content exists about you online.
The tool is powered by Claude AI (by Anthropic), which assesses your visibility across platforms based on your actual digital footprint. The ChatGPT and Perplexity results are informed simulations — Claude evaluates how those platforms would likely respond given what's publicly available about you online. The Claude result is a direct knowledge check: what Claude itself actually knows about you from its training data. For agents scoring under 40, the story is the same across all three platforms either way. And the Claude check is the most honest signal of true AI visibility you can get today.
Each scan runs live AI queries in real time, which has a cost. The daily limit keeps the tool free for everyone. Three searches per day is more than enough to scan yourself, a colleague, and a competitor in a single session. Your daily count resets at midnight.
GEO visibility doesn't change overnight. It takes weeks of consistent content, schema updates, and citation building before AI platforms start recognizing and citing an agent differently. Running the same scan daily would just return the same result and waste your searches. The 7-day window is the realistic minimum before meaningful change could appear. When a name is on cooldown, you can still view the full cached report from the last scan — useful for sharing with a client or referencing in a meeting.
Three things move the needle: citable content (blog posts, neighborhood guides, and market commentary that AI can read and attribute to you by name), structured data (schema markup on your website that tells AI exactly who you are, where you work, and what you specialize in), and citation signals (your name appearing consistently across review platforms, local directories, and industry sources).
